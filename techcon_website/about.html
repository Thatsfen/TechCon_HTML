History

TechCon began in the mid-1990s as a specialized conference focusing on power systems and utility maintenance, evolving over time to address broader tech innovations like IoT, cybersecurity, and sustainable energy. Originally aimed at engineers and utility professionals, it grew into a major networking and educational event, attracting tech firms, startups, and manufacturers. In recent years, TechCon adopted a hybrid format to expand its global reach, maintaining its relevance as a platform for exploring emerging technologies in energy and infrastructure.

Mission

The goals and driving principles of TechCon include:

  1. Promote Innovation: Showcase emerging technologies and solutions that drive advancements in energy management, infrastructure, and digital tools.
  2. Foster Collaboration: Create a platform for networking among engineers, tech firms, startups, and industry leaders.
  3. Encourage Knowledge Sharing: Provide educational workshops and hands-on demonstrations to empower professionals with the latest insights and best practices.
  4. Support Sustainability: Focus on sustainable energy solutions, smart grids, and environmentally friendly technologies for future infrastructure.
  5. Adaptability and Accessibility: Maintain relevance through a hybrid event format, ensuring global participation and inclusivity.
